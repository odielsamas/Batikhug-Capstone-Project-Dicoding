Batik Museum

Located in Pekalongan, Central Java

The Pekalongan Batik Museum was inaugurated by the 6th President of the Republic of Indonesia Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono on July 12 2006. The museum building has an area of ​​around 2500 m2 occupying a land area of ​​3675 m2. The building it occupies is a Dutch heritage building that has been established since 1906 which initially served as the financial administration office for a sugar factory located around the Pekalongan residential area. In its development, this building has changed its function several times as a City Hall, Mayor's Office, to the City Government office complex. The Pekalongan Batik Museum stores a large collection of old to modern batik, both from coastal areas, inland areas and other areas of Java, batik from various regions in the archipelago, such as from Sumatra, Kalimantan to Papua, and fabrics with batik techniques from foreign countries.

Not only exhibiting batik collections, the Pekalongan Batik Museum is also a batik training center and batik learning center. Students and general visitors can learn to make batik or conduct research on batik culture. The Pekalongan Batik Museum also has training programs/workshops for making batik directly to the public, to various schools, to various other institutions. Various collaborations with various parties are also carried out for the preservation of batik culture. All of these things are done in accordance with the Pekalongan Batik Museum's commitment to continue to maintain and preserve the cultural heritage of this noble ancestor.

Facilities in this batik museum:
1. Audio Visual Room
This room functions as a room for showing videos/films about the history of batik and the history of the Pekalongan Batik Museum.

2. Coastal Space / Showroom I
The Coastal Room displays a collection of batik from various regions on the north coast of Java, namely Madura, Lasem, Pati, Semarang, Pekalongan, Pemalang, Tegal, Brebes, Cirebon and Banten.

3. Nusantara Room / Showroom II
The Nusantara room displays a collection of batik from all regions/big cities in Indonesia which display the characteristics of their regional motifs/styles.

4. Interior Room / Showroom III
Interior Room is a collection room featuring interior batik from Solo, Yogyakarta. Inland batik is dominated by sogan colors and motifs according to standard.

5. Batik Museum Hall
The meeting room which can accommodate 80 people, can be used for various meetings, conferences, seminars, or banquets. The hall of the batik museum is also often used as a place for outreach, training, workshops held by the Pekalongan city government, the Central Java provincial government and the central government and the batik lover community.

6. Batik Museum Library
As a window of knowledge, the Batik Museum also provides a library which stores history books and reviews about batik in various languages. These books can be borrowed or read on the spot for learning or research on batik with a collection of more than 1292 batik books and general books. In addition, the existing telecenter facilities will increase your knowledge about batik.

7. Cafeteria
To meet the needs of visitors while relaxing and enjoying drinks and snacks, the Batik Museum provides a mini cafeteria which is located next to the batik workshop room.

8. Batik and Souvenir Shop
The Batik Shop is a facility at the Batik Museum that sells various kinds of batik products, the products sold at the batik shop are the products of the batik museum and batik entrepreneurs in the city of Pekalongan. Besides that, you can also get souvenirs such as wall hangings, key chains, pins, post cards, etc. that complement your visit to the Batik Museum.
